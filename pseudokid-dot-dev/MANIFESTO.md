It started with a dump.

A week-long sprint to export portfolio items from the Next.js site into a structured slide deck format. The intent was simple: feed it to Kimi Slides, send a slick deck to a prospect.

The resulting slide deck was surprisingly organized. Too good to just send and forget. The format had potential - it could showcase years of work in a way the Next.js site never did.

So I asked myself: "Why not build a full portfolio from this?"

That's how pseudokid-dot-dev was born.

---

FRAMING

Solo agency. Dev freelance. Focused on product building. A different direction from the old Next.js portfolio - not just a redesign, but a reframe.

---

DESIGN

Magazine. Zine-like. Intricate. Editorial. The kind of layout that draws you in and makes you stay.

But intricate layouts come with challenges:

- Multiple iterations between Google Stitch and code
- AI models struggling to implement pixel-perfect designs
- Responsiveness - the hardest part when every element is carefully placed
- Grid and masonry that had to work across breakpoints
- ASCII-fied images via canvas - a whole aesthetic on its own
- Carousels, thumbnail grids, single image variants - each deck mode needed love
- The home carousel (ImageTicker) that keeps things moving

---

THE STACK

Design: Google Stitch (Gemini Flash 3, Nano Banana)

Build: OpenCode (Kimi K2.5, MiniMax M2.5, Claude 4.6 Sonnet, Claude 4.6 Opus, Claude 4.5 Haiku, GPT 5.2 low, GPT 5.3 Codex)

The AI models did the heavy lifting - with guidance from Stitch and Flash. Iterations on iterations. Pixel by pixel.

---

DEPLOY

Vercel. Speed Insights. Analytics. Mixpanel.

All the tracking. All the insights.
